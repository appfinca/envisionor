FAQ

Q

Is Envisionor designed to replace the Medium Partner Program or Membership?

No. The Medium Partner Program is wonderful for serious writers who offer independent thoughts, and the Membership is an affordable way for general readers to access the thoughts while keeping the Medium running. However, sometimes the writers may just want to be helpful to their audience, and many readers are willing to pay for the insights that clear their doubts or solve their problems by hand. Envisionor bridges these two groups of users. Envisonor complements the Medium programs by offering incentives to public stories. So, writers can freely choose between publishing a member-only story and a public story.

Q

What are the fees?

An important mission of Envisionor is to get writers as much money as possible. This is why we have a fee of just 5% + $2 per story. Note that our partnering payment processor charges a $0.25 payout fee every time when a writer transfers funds out of her Evisionor account and toward a bank account. This is not listed in the fees breakdown within the writer's dashboard. For readers, the payment processor charges a 3% + $0.3 payment processing fee per pledge. We collect the fee in addition to the pledged money so to make sure that a writer can receive the expected amount of funds for a story. Readers who make multiple pledges within a month can have reduced fees. Please see the next FAQ for more details.

Q

Is there a way for backers to reduce the payment processing fees?

Yes. Evisionor periodically collects, at the beginning of each month, the funds pledged by a reader in a batch. If the reader makes multiple pledges that are collected by writers within a month, she only gets charged once and pays for the payment processing fee in proportion to the total amount. So, for active readers who create and back visions frequently, the payment processing fee can be reduced to an insignificant level. Note, however, that the batching cannot be applied to some situations, e.g., when the reader is deem to be fraudulent or when some writers have requested the up-front pay from their readers.

Q

Does a writer have to realize every vision suggested by the audience?

No. After a reader creates a vision and notifies a writer, the writer has an option to either accept or decline it. Only the visions accepted by the writer can be publicly shown on the writer's page. Even for those accepted visions, the writer has no obligation to realize them—she can freely choose what to write next depending on the funding status, interaction with readers or any other information.

Q

How do backers know if a vision will be realized?

When a writer starts working on topics suggested by some visions, she changes the state of the visions to “Working” and sets a soft deadline. Meanwhile, the writer starts collecting the pledges to support her work. Backers of the visions will be notified about this such that they can expect when a new story will be available.

Q

What is a writer obligated to do once collecting pledges from some backers?

Once collecting pledges, a writer is responsible for completing the story that covers the desired topics. At the same time, backers must understand that Envisonor is not a store. When making a vision, a reader helps create a new story—not ordering a copy that already exists. There is a chance something could happen that prevents the writer from being able to finish the story as promised. If a writer is absolutely unable to complete the story, he must make every reasonable effort to reach a satisfying conclusion with his backers, such as to refund the money to the backers.

Q

Can Envisionor refund the money to backers if they are not satisfied with a story they backed?

Since Envisionor only provides the venue upon which transactions between users are made, we do not issue refunds on behalf of writers. If a backer would like to request a refund, she will need to contact the writer in order to do so. Writers can issue refunds to their backers by clicking the “Refund” buttons on their Envisonor backer list of a story.

Q

What does Envisonor do to protect its community?

We hide from writers the chargeback fees, a very normal and expected business loss in the retail world. Defending against a chargeback often involves a tedious proof of service and the fees usually add up to a much higher total than the initial pledges, constituting a net loss for writers. Envisonor pays all associated fees for writers to protect them from the loss. However, if some writers get a lot of chargebacks, we may reach out to them to find out why and take action to save backer satisfaction.

We also do our best to fight against the frauds, although we are not able to completely eliminate it. Our Trust and Safety team monitors the system for suspicious activity. This team screen reports that are sent to us by our community and they take action if they find something that does not align with our rules.
