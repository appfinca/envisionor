FAQ

Q

Can a creator (YouTuber) have other revenue streams while using Envisionor?

Of course. A creator can have multiple revenue streams, such as running ads, offering membership, doing brand deals, or working with sponsors, as long as people (backers) whose funds were collected by the creator on Envisionor can see the video after it is published. If the video is release with restricted access (e.g., channel membership), the creator should inform the backers the link to the video and make sure they can watch the video by following the link. Envisionor offers tools that make it easy for the creator to share the video URL with backers.

Q

What are the fees?

An important mission of Envisionor is to get creators as much money as possible. This is why we have a fee of just 5% + $2 per video. Note that our partnering payment processor charges a $0.25 payout fee every time when a creator transfers funds out of her Evisionor account and toward a bank account. This is not listed in the fees breakdown within the creator's dashboard.

For backers, our payment processor charges a 3% + $0.3 payment processing fee per pledge. We collect the fee in addition to the pledged money so to ensure that a creator always receives the expected amount of funds. Backers who make multiple pledges within a month can have reduced fees. Please see the next FAQ for more details.

Q

Is there a way for backers to reduce the payment processing fees?

Yes. Evisionor periodically collects, at the beginning of each month, the funds pledged by a backer in a batch. If the backer makes multiple pledges that are collected by (different) creators within a month, she only gets charged once and pays for the payment processing fee in proportion to the total amount. So, for active backers who create and back visions frequently, the payment processing fee can be reduced to an insignificant level. Note, however, that the batching cannot be applied to some situations, e.g., when the backer's card is deem to be fraudulent or when some creators have requested the up-front pay from their audience.

Q

Does a creator have to accept and realize every vision suggested by her audience?

No. After a backer creates a vision, the creator can either accept or decline it. Only the visions accepted by the creator will be publicly shown on the creator's page. The creator has no obligation to realize a vision listed on her page unless she collects the pledged funds associated with the vision. If this happens, all backers of the vision will receive a notification telling that the creator has begun realizing the vision. See the next FAQ for details.

Q

How do backers know if a vision will be realized?

When a creator starts realizing a vision, she changes the state of the visions to In progress and sets an expected time of arrival (ETA) for the new video. All backers of the vision will be notified about the ETA. Meanwhile, the creator collects the pledged funds associated with the vision in order to support her work.

Q

What is a creator obligated to do once collecting pledges from backers?

Once collecting pledges, the creator is responsible for making videos that cover the desired scenes described by the backers. At the same time, backers must understand that Envisonor is not a store. By submitting a vision, a backer helps create something new; not ordering a video that already exists. There is a chance something could happen that prevents the creator from being able to finish filming as promised. If a creator is absolutely unable to deliver the video, he must make every reasonable effort to reach a satisfying conclusion with his backers, such as to refund the money to the backers via the creator's dashboard.

Q

Can Envisionor refund the money to backers if they are not satisfied with a video they backed?

Since Envisionor only provides the venue upon which transactions between users are made, we do not issue refunds on behalf of creators. If a backer would like to request a refund, she will need to contact the creator in order to do so. Creators can issue refunds to their backers by clicking the Refund buttons on their dashboard.

Q

What does Envisonor do to protect its community?

We hide from creators the chargeback fees, a very normal and expected business loss in the retail world. Defending against a chargeback often involves a tedious proof of service and the fees usually add up to a much higher total than the initial pledges, constituting a net loss for creators. Envisonor pays all associated fees for creators to protect them from the loss. However, if some creators get a lot of chargebacks, we may reach out to them to find out why and take action to save backer satisfaction.

We also do our best to fight against the frauds, although we are not able to completely eliminate it. Our Trust and Safety team monitors the system for suspicious activity. This team screen reports that are sent to us by our community and they take action if they find something that does not align with our rules.
